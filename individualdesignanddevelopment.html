    Week 1

  • Game Design

    • I have been working on an AR project from the Live Brief Design and Development module. I received a brief from Ashers Farm Sanctuary to help develop an app that would help increase donations, help with staff shortages and engagement with the animals on the farm. The brief led to an app being developed called the Ashers Farm Sanctuary Smart Guide that helps the visitors experience the farm through an AR driven environment.

    The app provided a digital tour to visitors that would also help educate them on the stories of the animals along with direct access to donations during the tour. The tour consisted of a physical marker in the form of an Image card that can be located in front of each animal's pen. When the image is scanned by the visitor, information about the animal in the pen along with donation options would appear in AR that would encourage donations and education via your smartphone.

    Research was done on the farm in the form of questionnaires answered by visitors to the farm. This research determined the most common operating system used on smartphones along with the average visitor age.

    The visitor age research conducted defined the look and feel of the models made for the application. Due to the younger age group the animals were given a toon feel rather than realistic. The other research conducted drove the platform of choice and technology stack that would be implemented during the development.

    The app was tested on the farm by visitors and received an overwhelmingly positive response. The client was very happy and the MVP was a success.

  • Individual Project Ideas

    • I have decided to continue developing the application during this module. I will be involved with Ashes Farm Sanctuary again this module to gain research towards the development and design direction of the application.

    During the development of the application this module I would like to implement a dialog system to the application so that the 3D models can communicate with the visitors and develop a dialog. This would be story driven like found in video games such as Mass Effect and would encourage engagement by the visitors by also featuring a small minigame where they get to feed the animal in the application.

    The farm itself becomes the playground and the story can help educate visitors on the animals and tell the story of the sanctuary and their vision. This holds a big advantage when it comes to educating visitors as indicated by [1] Dickey, M.D. study on Game Design Narrative for Learning: Appropriating Adventure Game Design Narrative Devices and Techniques for the Design of Interactive Learning Environments. The research shows that narrative will be able to support problem solving within the environments I create by educating users. Educating users through this way of dialog will blend the world around with the AR digital realm much better as the user associates the one with the other. If the dialog is used correctly I can blend the AR with the real to make the world created in the application feel more real and promote interaction by the users and increase education by doing rather than seeing.

    This goal will be achieved using the Unreal Engine and ARCore technologies along with the MVP I have already built and tested. I would like to achieve this goal by the end of this module and replicate the design from a single pen seamlessly to eight others. I would like to achieve this goal to be able to turn the farm into a digital AR flipbook that blends with reality while solving the problems that Ashers Farm Sanctuary face.

    Implementing the naterive would not be too complicated. I am using the plugin Blueprint DIalogs for the Unreal Engine to implement my dialogs into the application. By using this plugin it would help me quickly implement dialog and also leave room for voice over at a later stage. The dialogs will be written by Ashers Farm Sanctuary who has received a donation by a children's book writer to conduct the dialogs for the app.

    I will know I have reached this goal when the application can tell a small story at a location and provide interactive dialogs to the user. This will include a small mini game that will encourage interaction with the real world pen by the user to determine answers to questions.

    I will know that I am moving forward when I see my sprints that are set out completed on time and the application taking shape as the animals' dialogs appear. This can be achieved due to the stability of the MVP developed in the previous module. I already have all the models and animations and need to put everything together and integrate the dialog system which I am familiar with already and have integrated into projects in the past.

    Given the time set out for the project to be completed I do believe I have enough time. I will be breaking the project down into sprints which will drive the development of the project. This leaves room for change and flexibility by using Agile Methodologies to drive development. In the past I have run into barriers that involved the shutdown of production due to unforeseen circumstances. In South Africa we are experiencing Load Shedding which shuts off the power in cities for three hours at a time twice a day. Being Agile is perfect for handling unforeseen situations as indicated by [2]. Stefan H Thomke; Donald G Reinertsen; did a study on Agile product development which featured the managing and development flexibility in uncertain environments.

    Due to the instability of the country atm and moving forward, me and the client have agreed to continue with the agile sprint method for development to keep things moving no matter the circumstances. Staying flexible with agile would also help reduce the need for accurate long-term forecasts as we are not sure what could happen in the coming weeks.

    The study introduces the notion of development flexibility and found that projects using agile outperformed projects using inflexible technologies by a factor of 2.2 in extreme circumstances and when under pressure. This is of huge value to the project and will help things move forward even when circumstances are not favorable.

    Week 2

  • Fictional Worlds & Game Mechanics

    • In week two of the module I was given an exercise to Create a game. The brief asked me to imagine how you could build a game based on the character's everyday life when it suddenly takes a dramatic turn while the situation changes suddenly and the person is forced to step up and go beyond what they thought was humanly possible. Luckily, they have your trusty app to give them extra superpowers.

  • Mechanics

    • Point and click adventure game filled with puzzles. The puzzles consist of bringing images to life or drawing new ones to enter the world to overcome obstacles in the characters life.

  • Summery

    • David Muller is an Illustrator that takes life day by day. He is always on time for work and leaves at exactly the right moment. He works for a Game Development company and his day is mainly made up of drawing Illustrations of Characters that will be integrated into video games developed by the company.

    During one normal day, David finishes work and heads over to his car. As he reaches the door of the vehicle he notices that someone broke into his car. As David sighs and starts looking around for what the thieves took. He hears a voice telling him to turn around. Two thieves hold him up with a knife and force his wallet and keys out of his pocket.

    As David tried to stop them his bag with his drawings fell to the ground and the drawings started to come alive. As the characters of his dark mind tore the thieves limb from limb he realised that he has been granted the ability to bring creations to life.

    As the burglars lay lifeless on the ground the characters go back into the page and become drawings again. With his new found ability he sets out to experience life with his new found ability.

  • Deliverable

The first chapter will deliver a single boss at the end of the episode and it would hold around 3 hours worth of content to drive the story. It will release at a reduced price to kick start the episodic series.

    Ashers Farm Sanctuary Website Link

  • Colour Palette

    • I generated a colour pallette for the application based on the image received from the questionnaire and found some similarities in the colours found. The colours feature a brown and dark palette. This can affect the applications immersion as indicated by a study [1] done on “Regarding color psychology principles in adventure games to enhance the sense of immersion” done by Samad Roohi in 2019. It states that players generally experience immersion by paying no attention to the surrounding events and that if the environment is in a well-designed form, graphic design elements can enhance the level of players involvement in a game.

    Getting the player to immerse into the experience is a key aspect of the application. I want to blent the real with the unreal very closely to create an amazing experience for the clients and create an environment to learn. By applying the correct color and understanding my colour palette better in the future I can make all my applications more immersive. Exploring the different photos and colors in this experiment has taught me the importance of the relationship between colour and immersion.

  • Colour Composition

    • The Principles for making 3 colors look like 4…

    The experiment is driven by a study done by [2] UNSW school of Physics in Sydney Australia. In the experiment I mix and blend colours together to achieve more colours then are actively displayed on the screen.

    Three findings that I applied to my experiment to determine the amount of colours generated when combining them were as follows based on [3] 'Colour', How to use colour in art and design by Anderson Feiser.

    In the findings it states that A color surrounded by a darker color will appear to be lighter than it is–and–conversely, a color surrounded by a lighter color will appear to be darker than it is. A secondary or tertiary color surrounded by one of it’s parent colors will look more like the opposite parent. e.g. brown surrounded by light brown looks Dark brown. A color surrounded by a duller version of the same hue will look brighter and–and–a color surrounded by a brighter version of the same hue will look duller.

    I do think that the experiment was of value. It was the first time I have played with colour and I have gained knowledge from generating the colour pallette of how to apply colour to my textures inside my application. The results found would drive the palette of all designs featured in the application moving forward.

    I will need to adjust the GUI to match the colour pallette found. I want to have all the colours of the application sync up and match a specific palette so help make the application feel more polished and complete.

    [4] Rider, Rose M study on "Color Psychology and Graphic Design Applications" states that cool hues are relaxants and generally preferred over their more arousing warm counterparts. I will remember to apply this to my GUI and settings to help sooth the player into the experience in the future. It also states that color can advance or devastate design effectiveness and have massive economic implications for companies and products when getting colour wrong in the application as users feel alienated or even disconnected with what they are experiencing. I want to avoid this in the future as one again immersion and engagement are of the utter most importance if the application was going to educate users and encourage learning through engagement.
